# Monetization and rights

## Current YouTube target

For watch-page advertising revenue, plan around:

- 1,000 subscribers
- 4,000 valid public watch hours in the previous 12 months

Alternatively, a channel can qualify with 1,000 subscribers and 10 million valid public Shorts views in 90 days. Shorts-feed watch time does not count toward the 4,000-hour threshold.

Meeting the numbers does not guarantee acceptance. YouTube reviews the whole channel for originality, authenticity, policy compliance, and rights.

## Silent gameplay risk

YouTube states that videos simply showing extended gameplay may not be accepted for monetization. Nintendo’s guidelines also ask creators to add creative or editorial input rather than upload mere gameplay copies.

For this channel, every episode should therefore demonstrate original authorship through:

- A planned completion route
- A specific episode objective
- Original progress and checklist overlays
- Instructional route or item notes
- Meaningful chapter structure
- Purposeful removal of dead time
- A results screen proving progress
- Distinct titles, thumbnails, and descriptions

This reduces risk but does not guarantee YPP approval. Review the format after Episodes 8 and 13.

## Nintendo guidelines

- YouTube Partner Program monetization is among Nintendo’s permitted platform monetization methods.
- Do not imply Nintendo sponsorship or affiliation.
- Use gameplay generated after a game’s official release.
- Use only legally obtained game software.
- Do not upload Nintendo trailers, music, artwork collections, or other mere copies as standalone content.
- Check the current guidelines again before applying to YPP.

## Emulator disclosure

Use this language when useful:

> Played through emulation using a legally obtained copy of the original Nintendo 64 game. No cheats or save states were used.
